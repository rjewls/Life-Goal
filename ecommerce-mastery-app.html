📸 Phone Photography Masterclass

The $0 Setup

1
Lighting (FREE - Use Natural Light)
  • Best time: 9-11 AM or 3-5 PM (soft indirect sunlight)
  • Best location: Near window, product 1-2 meters from window
  • Avoid: Direct harsh sunlight (creates hard shadows)
  • Cloudy days: Actually perfect! Clouds = natural softbox
  • DIY Reflector: White paper/cardboard on opposite side of window (bounces light, fills shadows)
2
Background ($5-15)
  • Option 1: White poster board (500-1000 DA) - Clean, professional
  • Option 2: Solid color fabric/bedsheet - Lifestyle feel
  • Option 3: Wooden table/floor - Natural, organic vibe
  • Option 4: Marble contact paper (1500 DA) - Premium look
  • Pro tip: Keep background out of focus (tap product to focus phone camera)
3
Phone Camera Settings
  • ✅ Clean your lens first (obvious but forgotten!)
  • ✅ Use main camera, NOT selfie camera (better quality)
  • ✅ Tap product to focus and set exposure
  • ✅ Use grid lines (Settings → Camera → Grid) for alignment
  • ✅ Never use digital zoom - move closer physically
  • ✅ Portrait mode for blurred background (if available)
  • ✅ Shoot in natural light, increase brightness in editing (not on camera)

5 Essential Product Photo Angles

1️⃣ Hero Shot

Angle: 45° from above, product centered
Use: Main Marketplace thumbnail, first Instagram photo
Tip: Clean background, all product visible, well-lit

2️⃣ In-Use Action

Angle: Hands holding/using product

3️⃣ Detail Close-Up

Angle: Very close to show texture/quality
Use: Carousel post slide 2-3, proving authenticity
Tip: Focus on logo, material, craftsmanship

4️⃣ Size Comparison

Angle: Product next to common object (phone, hand, coin)

Tip: Use universally known size reference

5️⃣ Lifestyle Context

Angle: Product in natural setting (kitchen, desk, bedroom)
Use: Instagram feed, aspirational content
Tip: Clean background, product in natural use case

6️⃣ Before/After

Angle: Split screen or 2 photos side-by-side
Use: Problem/solution products (cleaners, organizers, beauty)
Tip: Dramatic difference, same lighting for both

🎣 Scroll-Stopping Hook Formulas

First 1 second determines if they watch or scroll. Master these 10 hooks.

The 1-Second Rule: Users decide to watch or scroll in 0.5-1 second. Your hook must create curiosity, shock, or promise value INSTANTLY.
1. Pattern Interrupt

"Nobody talks about this..."

2. Problem Callout
"Tired of [problem]?"
"If you struggle with [X]..."

"When [relatable problem]..."

Why it works: Immediate relatability, self-selects audience

3. Curiosity Gap
"The secret nobody tells you..."
"Wait for the end!"
"Before you buy [product]..."
"3 things I wish I knew..."

Why it works: Creates information gap brain wants to close

4. Social Proof
"Everyone's buying this..."
"Sold 50 in 2 days..."
"My mom asked where I got this"
"Viral on TikTok for a reason"

Why it works: FOMO + bandwagon effect

5. Transformation Promise
"Before vs After using this..."
"This changed my [routine]"
"From [bad state] to [good state]"
"How I went from [X] to [Y]"

Why it works: Shows clear benefit, aspirational

6. Myth Busting
"You're using this wrong..."
"Stop buying [product], get this"
"The truth about [product]"
"[Common belief] is a lie"

Why it works: Challenges existing knowledge, educates

7. Urgency/Scarcity
"Last 5 in stock..."
"Ends tonight!"
"Don't miss this..."
"Running out fast!"

Why it works: Fear of missing out triggers action

8. Price Anchor
"Costs [high $] but worth it..."
"Cheaper than [alternative]"
"[Price] for THIS?!"
"Save [amount] by doing this"

Why it works: Creates value perception contrast

9. Relatability/POV
"POV: You're too lazy to [task]"
"When you finally find..."
"Tell me you [trait] without..."
"Me after discovering this"

Why it works: "That's literally me!" engagement

10. Question Hook
"Why don't more people know?"
"How is this only [price]?"
"Where have you been?!"
"What if I told you..."

Why it works: Brain automatically tries to answer questions

🧠 Customer Psychology Shortcuts

Principle: People fear losing more than they enjoy gaining

Bad: "Get 20% off"
Good: "Don't miss 20% off - ends tonight!"
Better: "You're losing [benefit] every day without this"
2. Social Proof (Bandwagon Effect)

Principle: People follow what others do

Bad: "High-quality product"
Good: "50 sold this week"
Better: "[Customer name] and 49 others bought this week"
3. Reciprocity (Give First, Get Back)

Principle: People feel obligated to give back when they receive

Action: Give free value first (how-to guide, tips) → People feel indebted → More likely to buy
Example: "Free guide: 5 Ways to [solve problem]" → Then pitch product
4. Anchoring (Price Perception)

Principle: First price you see becomes your reference point

Bad: "3000 DA"
Good: "5000 DA → 3000 DA"
Better: "Normally 5000 DA → Today ONLY 3000 DA (Save 2000!)"
5. Authority (Expert Positioning)

Principle: People trust experts

Action: Position yourself as knowledgeable:
• Share tips/hacks in your niche
• Post educational content
• Answer questions publicly
→ People see you as expert → More likely to buy from you

🚀 Scaling Rules

How to increase budgets without breaking performance

📈
❌ The #1 Scaling Mistake

Doubling your budget overnight. This resets the algorithm's learning and usually tanks performance.

1
The 20% Rule

Never increase budget more than 20% at a time. Wait 2-3 days between increases to let the algorithm stabilize.

2
Horizontal Scaling

Instead of increasing one ad set budget, duplicate winning ad sets with the same budget. More stable than vertical scaling.

3
When to Scale

Only scale campaigns that have been profitable for at least 3-4 days consecutively. One good day isn't enough data.

4
Know When to Stop

Every campaign has a ceiling. If ROAS drops below profitable after scaling, pull back. Not every winner scales infinitely.

🧮 Break-Even ROAS Formula

Break-Even ROAS = 1 ÷ Profit Margin

Anything above that ROAS is profit. Below it, you're losing money on ads.

📸 Step 4: UGC Photography & Visual Strategy

Professional-looking UGC doesn't require expensive equipment. Follow these proven strategies for high-converting visual content.

🎯 The UGC Visual Formula

Rule: Your visuals should look like they were shot by a real customer, NOT a professional studio. Authenticity > Polish.

1
Lighting Setup (Natural Light Wins)

Best: Film near a window during golden hour (1hr after sunrise, 2hrs before sunset). Soft, diffused natural light = most authentic look.

Avoid: Harsh overhead lights, yellow indoor bulbs. If indoors at night, use ring light on lowest setting + window light.

2
Background & Setting

Best Backgrounds: Clean kitchen counter, coffee shop table, bedroom nightstand, car interior, outdoors at park.

Rule: Show some life - coffee mug in frame, laptop, plants. Too clean = fake. Slightly messy = relatable.

3
Camera Angles That Convert

Top Performers:
• POV angle (filming your own hands using product)
• Slightly above eye level (most flattering)
• Close-up on product details
• Over-the-shoulder while using product

Avoid: Tripod-stable shots (too professional). Add slight natural shake.

4
Movement & Energy

What Works: Quick transitions, product reveals, unboxing shots, hand gestures while talking.

Pacing: Change shot every 2-3 seconds max. Static shots = scroll away. Keep it dynamic but natural.

5
Audio Strategy

TikTok/IG: Use trending sounds (check Creative Center). Layer your voiceover at 80% volume, trending audio at 20-30%.

Facebook: Voiceover only works best. Add captions (80% watch on mute).

6
Editing Style

Tools: CapCut (free, best for UGC), InShot, or built-in TikTok editor.

Style: Minimal effects. Add text overlays for key points, subtle zoom for emphasis. Use native platform features (TikTok text styles, IG stickers).

⚡ Pro Tips for High-Converting Visuals
  • First Frame = Everything: Show product + face + emotion in first 0.5 seconds
  • Show Results Fast: Before/after within first 5 seconds
  • Use Your Hands: Point, hold product, interact. Makes it feel real
  • Imperfections Sell: Slightly shaky camera, natural pauses, "um's" and "like's" = authentic
  • Text on Screen: Reinforce your hook with text overlay (different font than platform default)
  • End with Product Shot: Last 2 seconds = clear product + logo/brand name
